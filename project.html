5. Design and developed an admin interface to allow administrators to manage the database and view logs

Risk analysis tool

The Risk Analysis Tool project aims to address the aftermath of a significant ransomware attack on Debt-IN Consultants, a debt recovery solutions partner based in South Africa. The attack resulted in a data breach compromising consumer and employee personal information, posing severe risks to the organization and individuals affected.Steps Taken:

1. Incident Analysis: Conducted a detailed analysis of the ransomware attack incident at Debt-IN Consultants, assessing potential impact on affected parties, and vulnerabilities exploited by the attackers.
2. Methodology Selection: Selected the NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology) framework as the risk analysis methodology for evaluating the security risks associated with the data breach.
3. Tool Design: Designed the Risk Analysis Tool to provide a comprehensive assessment of the security risks, incorporating elements such as risk assessment, risk matrix, inherent risk analysis, and mitigation strategies.
4. Data Collection: Gathered relevant data related to the ransomware attack, including information on the compromised systems, types of data exposed, attack vectors, and security controls in place.
5. Risk Assessment: Utilized the qualitative risk assessment approach within the NIST framework to determine the risk level associated with the data breach, considering factors such as impact, likelihood, and vulnerability.
6. Mitigation Strategies: Identified and proposed mitigation strategies to address the identified security risks, including measures such as enhancing cybersecurity controls, implementing incident response procedures, and enhancing employee training and awareness.
7. Tool Implementation: Implemented the Risk Analysis Tool to analyze the data breach scenario at Debt-IN Consultants, generating risk assessments, visualizations, and actionable recommendations for stakeholders.
